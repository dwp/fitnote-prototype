You’ll be told during your application for Employment and Support Allowance (ESA) or ‘new style’ ESA, when you need to send fit notes (sometimes called ‘sick notes’ or a ‘statement of fitness for work’).

You'll need:

  • your fit note signed by your doctor
  • your National Insurance number
  • a phone or tablet with a camera, or a computer with a saved photo or copy of your fit note

You do not need to send a fit note if you cannot work while you are self-isolating because of coronavirus (COVID-19).

This service is also available in Welsh (Cymraeg).

What is a fit note?

Doctors issue fit notes to people to provide evidence of the advice they have given about their fitness for work. This service can only accept fit notes, an example of a fit note can be seen in the below image.

! Warning This service does not accept or process med 10s, isolation notes or letters from medical professionals.
Do you not have a fit note?
If you have evidence from a medical professional that you are unable to work, but it is not a fit note, it may be one of the following:
  • Med 10
  • Isolation note
  • A letter from a medical professional
    • If you require a fit note, please arrange an appointment with your doctor.

    Other ways to apply

    You can post your fit note. If you need to send any other medical evidence, you must post it.

    Send your fit note or other medical evidence to:

    Freepost DWP ESA 26
    Posting a fit note
    Fit notes can be posted directly to ESA using the address Freepost DWP ESA 26. You do not need a postage stamp.
